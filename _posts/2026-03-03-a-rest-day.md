Today is a rest day.  Not so much that we need a rest, but more that my legs are so sun-burnt that I can’t walk.  Not too clever, I know - sun cream went everywhere *except* my calves, and that’s where the sun attacked me!  We had such a lovely day yesterday, in the sun, on the beach and in the sea.  
  
![cabbage beach]({{"/assets/images/cabbage_beach.png"|relative_url}})  
  
We walked to Cabbage Beach, on the north side of Paradise Island, by way of (another) Starbucks for (another) Cold Brew and covered the best part of 20,000 steps getting there.  Access to the beach wasn’t obvious - there’s a lot of building work about to happen - and signage was less than obvious.  Thank goodness for Apple Maps!  It was worth the struggle; this beach was glorious, and, by walking a little way from the entrance, quite empty, with perhaps 10 other people within 100s of metres.  The sea was inviting: blue and clear and not too cold.  Bianca was soon swimming and, with a little encouragement (😋) so was I.  
  
![swimming in the sea]({{"/assets/images/in_the_sea.png"|relative_url}})  
  
We sauntered off back down the beach just after midday to look for a place for lunch.  Eventually, at the base of the bridge back to New Providence, we stumbled upon “Carnivale”, an excellent restaurant cum bar with a great seafood menu.  Have you ever tried Conch?  
  
![mark and bianca at carnivale]({{"/assets/images/carnivale.png"|relative_url}})  
  
After eating, we set off for the walk home.  Oh my God, was it hot!  Too hot.  Not wanting to go everywhere by taxi, we chose to hut down a local bus, or Jitney.  There are about 23 different routes, with A and (sometimes) B variants.  Fortunately 3 buses arrived together, so we were able to ask a driver which route would take us closed to where we needed to be.  We jumped on the 9B, together with about 8 other people, half filling the bus.  This is an experience!  These Jitney fly about the streets at high speed, with music playing so loud conversation is practically impossible!  The driver was great!  After what seemed to be a tour of most of the east of the island, he dropped us off within a couple of minutes from where we are staying.  All for $2 a-piece.  
  
![back of the bus]({{"/assets/images/back_of_the_bus.png"|relative_url}})  
  
It was then that the sun-burn kicked in, which brings us to the top of this post 😬.
