![Gordon’s bar]({{"/assets/images/gordons.png"|relative_url}})  
  
Thursday, South Long Island.  
  
Having a car, we could search a little further for breakfast.  There’s really only one road on Long Island, much like Exuma.  We trundle along, not really knowing how fast we’re going - remember, the speedo doesn’t work - looking for some place to go.  We’ve been told that the Greek Cafe is good, so now we just need to find it, once we’ve found an ATM (‘cause cash is king on Long Island).    
  
There it is! We pull up in the parking lot (well, gravel area to one side) and saunter in.  As usual, it’s full of just us.  We take a table and meet up with Marcia, the lovely lady that runs the place with her husband.  He’s Greek, she’s Bahamian.  Aside from the excellent food, we have great conversation for an hour or more.  Occasionally, other people come in, usually for take out, perhaps join the conversation for a little while, and the leave. We get on very well with Marcia and, as we went there for breakfast again this morning, she and Bianca are now WhatsApp buddies!  

![Inside Gordon’s bar]({{"/assets/images/at_the_bar.png"|relative_url}})

During our chat with Marcia, Bianca mentions that we are planning to go to Gordon’s Beach.  Great idea, says Marcia.  Be sure to go to the bar, there, right on the beach.  Just drive until the road runs out, then you’ll be in the right place.  This is the most south-easterly point on the Island.    

![us at the bar]({{"/assets/images/us_at_gordons.png"|relative_url}})

After some time out of the intense mid-day sun, we set off. It takes about an hour.  Again, we don’t know how fast we’re are driving, but with the bumps, missing asphalt, and curb-less shoulders, we don’t drive too quickly.    

![Sunset through the window at Gordon’s bar]({{"/assets/images/sunset_gordons_window.png"|relative_url}})

We get to the beach and… wow, it’s beautiful.  We find a vacant pair of loungers - there are (may be) eight, with fewer people on the beach.  We put our bags down and,  before relaxing in the sun, we just pop into the bar to see how it’s like.  We don’t go back to the beach until sundown!  This was a bar out of a movie.  Aside from perhaps one table, everyone sat up at the 3-sided bar, with Earnest, the proprietor stood in the middle. There we three guys who’d sailed from New Zealand (and we totally drunk), and two American couples who’d sailed had been sailing for one year and four years respectively.  They all had great stories to tell, and Bianca was pulling every yarn from each of them.  Meanwhile, Earnest was plying us with his “World’s best” rum punch - probably true - and then various spirits of wild and exotic origins.  What an evening!  We left as the sun went down and, once we’d figured out how to turn the car’s headlamps on, headed back on the road with no turns, to Gems on Paradise.  

![Gordon’s beach at sunset]({{"/assets/images/gordons_beach.png"|relative_url}})
  
By the way, we think we shared our room with a couple of frogs last night.  There was definitely one on the bath this morning, but I’m not sure if that was the same one as the one under the bed that night before 😬🐸.
