title: "Car hire experience"  
date:  2026-03-11 18:43:38 -0400  
---  
Getting around the islands needs a car.  Only New Providence has an effective bus service; elsewhere, other than for the odd trip, a rental is quite necessary.  
  
Travelling up and down Long Island really needs a car; the Boat Club provides a list of ‘approved’ vendors.  The first issue is having anyone answer the phone when you call.  We can through 5 or 6 numbers before having someone pick up the phone!  The conversation migrates to WhatsApp to confirm arrangements.  
  
Our car, a new 2017 Jeep Compass, comes with a wide range of sophisticated features, including Range Anxiety and Speed Uncertainty.  Each time the car is started, a tiny Japanese person secreted in the glove box announces “something” before trying to hammer their way out of the dash.  They don’t try for too long, though they endeavour again each quarter-hour or so, as we drive along.  The instrument panel is equipped with more illuminations that I am used to, with the brake light, engine light, ABS warning and traction warning light all brightly glowing, accompanied by the occasional oil light and a musical beeb each minute or so.  This is normal, but you are left wondering whether the car is going to make it to where ever you’re destined before seizing up completely.  To add to the excitement, the speedometer doesn’t seem to register any forward motion and the tachometer moved only an additional mile over a 35 mile trip.  The 81,000 miles it registered is likely a little understated.   
  
Our destination, when we finally did get there, is Gems at Paradise Beach, a small, beautiful country hotel where it seems only we are staying.  We have a wing to ourselves.  The proprietor tells me that his own vehicle has many of the same advanced features.  Modern cars need sophisticated workshops and computer systems to right them - a tiny island in the Bahamas has none of these things.  It’s normal - ignore the illuminations and call the rental company if the car really does break down.
