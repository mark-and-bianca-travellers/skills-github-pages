Getting about New Providence has been about walking and catching local buses (with the odd taxi thrown-in, where unavoidable).    
  
In the morning, before and for a little while after sunrise, it’s been cool enough to walk.  Any time closer to the middle of the day *definitely *requires transportation!  Coming back from Downtown on Monday, we discovered the local bus, and in particular, route 9a.  
  
Timmy (we later learnt the driver’s name) is the only 9a driver in the only 9a bus.  After dropping us close to home on Monday, he let us know he’d be passing by the same place ‘just after’ 7, 8 and 9 am on subsequent days.  On Wednesday, we went to give it a go, to travel back to Downtown.    
  
![waiting for the bus]({{"/assets/images/waiting.png"|relative_url}})  
  
We walked to the ‘stop’ for 9 am and waited.  It’s a beautiful view.  Blue skies, blue sea and hot. Very hot 🥵.  We were not in a hurry.  We stared down the road, waiting for the 9a to roll up.  Perhaps we had the times wrong?  Perhaps the route was different on Wednesdays?  Hey look, says Bianca after an hour or so, here’s a bus going the other way, coming up from behind us!  It was Timmy!  He slowed, took the corner behind us (off any route we were expecting) and open the doors to pick us up.    
  
![through the front window]({{"/assets/images/front_window.png"|relative_url}})  
  
This sure is a wild schedule! And clearly a close-to-random route.  Whatever; we were glad he’d arrived, and we set off on a ‘scenic’ route to Downtown.  
  
These buses are not big - perhaps 20 seated passengers - seeming to stop just about anywhere any passenger wants, each passing the driver $1.50 / $2.00 as they leave.  Every now and again, Timmy would see someone he knew, perhaps crossing the road or waiting on the sidewalk; with his window open he’d holler a greeting and sound his horn (as he’d later do when he saw Bianca and I walking along to Starbucks, first thing in the morning).  Eventually we arrive after an exciting journey, seeing places we certainly wouldn’t have, had we have sped along in a cab.  
  
![a bus]({{"/assets/images/bus.png"|relative_url}})  
  
Returning home also turned out differently to our expectations.  We stood at the street where we had been dropped-off, waiting for a 9a to arrive.  Eventually, after perhaps an hour, a driver from another route called over to us.  The 9a - here we learnt that there was only one bus - wasn’t running!  I can take you as far as (half-way) he said, and from there we should get a taxi.  Ok; we jumped on and headed off, the door open as we went, to keep us cool.  
  
![open bus door]({{"/assets/images/open_door.png"|relative_url}})  
  
After what seemed only a few minutes and a handful of stops, he stopped again and called down the bus.  “Do we want to take a cab from here?” We stood-up, he jumped down from the bus and grabbed a taxi driver that was next to his cab at the side of the road. “Take these folks where they need to go!”  And with that, he left with his bus and we travelled home in the cab.    
  
How about that for service!
