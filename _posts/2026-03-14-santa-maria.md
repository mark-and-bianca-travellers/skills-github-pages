You know those scenes in multiple Bond movies, where someone with an incredible body walks slowly out of a bright blue sea on to impossibly white sand?  Well, if that wasn’t filmed here, it really could have been.  

![A beach out of a Bond movie]({{"/assets/images/bond_beach.png"|relative_url}})
  
We’re situated in one of a handful of beach bungalows and villas, just a stone’s throw from the sea, with a veranda that lets us look out at the surf and the five or six sale-boats anchored in the bay.  Aside from the tiny flying creatures that are biting our legs - so many more than we’ve encountered elsewhere - this is close to perfect.  We’re swimming in the clear and warm waters either side of midday, drinking plenty (coffee and juice) and talking about our plans for next week.

![2026-03-14-Santa Maria boardwalk and beach]({{"/assets/images/santa_maria_beach.png"|relative_url}})
  
This, according to some, is where Columbus first encountered the New World.  This morning we drove for a few minutes to the monument that commemorates that moment, marking the beginning of the end of the native Lucayan people.  Due to their great beauty and ability to dive deep for pearls, the whole population was lost to the slave trade, leaving these islands empty of people for more than 100 years.

![Columbus monument]({{"/assets/images/monument.png"|relative_url}})
  
After a little bit of historical reality, we returned to our dream-like hotel for breakfast before going for a swim.  With a little pile of our towels and phones as a marker on the beach, we set about swimming across the bay.  Yesterday had taught us that this was no mean feat, as the current was moving left to right, and we were trying to swim right to left!  After quite some effort, we did it!

![Bianca choosing from the menu]({{"/assets/images/breakfast_choice.png"|relative_url}})
  
Now, having rested out of the hottest sun, it’s time for a coffee and another visit to the sea.
