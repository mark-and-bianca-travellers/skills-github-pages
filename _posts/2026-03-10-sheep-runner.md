On Monday we said goodbye to Exuma, to leave on the “Sheep Runner” for Long Island. In the past, Long Island was where goats and sheep were farmed, to be brought over to Exuma to feed the growing population.  Needless to say, there was no evidence of any livestock on the spotless boat that whisked us away from Government Harbour to Salt Ponds and the Long Island Harbour Club, where we are currently staying.  
  
![Long Island Harbour Club]({{"/assets/images/LIHC.png"|relative_url}})  
  
But before all that…   
  
On Sunday we hired a car so that we could take ourselves about the island. As we had the car until early afternoon on Monday (with our day-long hire lasting a little longer than 24 hours) we took the opportunity to visit another beach or two that we had missed on Sunday. Exuma Point, in particular, we’d been told was quite beautiful, and so it was. We walked over a seemingly endless beach, through blue shallows to a gorgeous sandbar; slowly strolling about enjoying the increasingly warming sunshine. Quite heavenly.   
  
![The beach at Exuma Point]({{"/assets/images/exuma_point.png"|relative_url}})  
  
Back into the Jeep - left-hand drive but on roads like the U.K. - we made our way back to Regatta Point. We’d agreed to leave the keys with Nancy (who the hire company knew), so we parked up, struggled to close the ragtop of the car, before enjoying our terrace for one last time.   
The Sheep Runner was leaving from a dock less than 50 metres from Nancy’s, so we walked over at the allotted time, wheeling our bags. The boat’s captain took our bags and packed them away, whilst we took our seats with the other eight passengers for the exhilarating 1 and 3/4 hour trip to Long Island.
