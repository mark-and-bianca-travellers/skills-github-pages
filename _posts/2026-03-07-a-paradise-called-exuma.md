It’s the end of our second full day on Exuma; it’s been wonderful and we’re exhausted.  There’ll be another post about what we’ve been up to today, with pigs and iguanas (yes, really) but first, this magical place.  
  
We’re at Regatta Point in Georgetown, staying in a wonderful apartment - lounge, kitchen, bathroom, bedroom and two terraces immediately next to the sea - owned by the lovely Nancy, who came here with her husband in the 60s from San Francisco, and never went back.
  
![inside Regatta Point]({{"/assets/images/regatta_point_lounge.png"|relative_url}})  
  
To say it’s quaint would do it a disservice; it is totally relaxing, with wooden floors and walls and ceiling, no TV and the patchiest of WiFi.  There’s a constant sound of the sea and the (very) occasional boat racing by.  We’re here for only four nights, but I think we both wish it was longer.  
  
Georgetown is the capital of Exuma, and we are a couple of minutes stroll from Downtown - one hotel, one coffee shop, one apparel store and one school.  I think you get what it’s like. Breakfast and sometimes lunch too is in the coffee shop, that does an excellent omelette with fried potatoes and a mean meat pizza. The hotel - the Peace and Plenty - has a good restaurant (you need to book) where you can get good lobster and excellent red snapper.  We had our first evening meal there; the food was great and the guests fascinating!
  
![Peace and Plenty Hotel]({{"/assets/images/peace_plenty.png"|relative_url}})  
  
Tomorrow we plan to be out and about in the little Jeep we’ve hired.  Left-hand drive in a right-hand drive world: should be interesting!
